Module

Briefly, concisely and accurately

We can learn from the reading that Reading-Fact. Three theories are proposed in the reading Reading-point, while all of them are of refuted by the lecture, which states that Listening-Point.

First, the reading claims that R1-theory, as R1-reason. However, the lecture does not agree with the idea and claims that L1-reason.

Second, the reading indicates that R2-theory, which is reflected by R2-reason. Nevertheless, the lecture counteracts the idea: L2-reason.

Third, the reading demonstrates that R3-reason, since R3-reason. Still, the lecture argues against such opinion-L3-reason.

Example

We can learn from the reading that a large, oval-shape ceramic vessel with intricate carvings was discovered in central Italy, possibly dating back to the 4th century BCE. Three theories are proposed in the reading rega rding its purpose, while all of them are refuted by the lecture, which states that all these theories have flaw and non can be definitely proven.

First, the reading claims that the vessel was used in religious ceremonies as the carvings on the vessel depict scenes of offering deities, possibly showing the purpose of a container for animal sacrifice to gods for blessing. However, the lecture does not agree with the idea and claims that it was common to illustrate culture narratives on everyday items, denying the religious purpose of the vessel. For example, many common items used today, such as vases and teapots, come with depictions of historical and mythical characters and events.

Second, the reading indicates that the vessel was used for storing food and drink, which is reflected in historical records and findings and suggested by the large size and sturdy construction of the vessel. Nevertheless, the lecture counteracts the idea: the intricate carvings on the vessel required great effort, and it was unlikely for people to use such an item for mundane purpose. In addition, the vessel was discovered in pristine condition, showing no wear and tear from daily handling.

Third, the reading demonstrates that the vessel might have been used as a funerary object since similar vessels were found in other burial sites. Furthermore, the intricate carvings on the vessel are highly decorative, and they possibly represented homage to the deceased. Still, the lecture argues against such opinion- the number of this type of pottery is too small to prove wide funerary use. Besides, recent discoveries show that funerary objects in that part of Italy were almost all stone tools so the locals might have actually preferred practical items ovel symbolic ones for funerary objects.

Academic Discussion

Question Types

  • Evaluation 40% discuss the advantage/disadvantage of one thing(whether it is good)
  • Comparison 30% dicuss the benefit/harm of two thing(which one is better)
  • Solution 15% give a solution to some problem(a good/better answer)
  • Argument 15% if you are approve the view, and why(whether it is right)

Topics

  • Education 25%
  • Work & Business 20%
    1. Profit Maximazation-Generate revenue & reduce costs to ensure profitablity
    2. Growth & Expansion-Increase market share & develop new market
    3. Better Products & Customer Satisfaction-Provide products/services better than competitors
    4. Improvement & Innovation-Continuously improve and create new products & services
    5. Employee Well-being & Satisfaction 6 Social & Enviromental Responsibility
  • Environment 15%
  • Tech 15%
  • Urban Development 15%
  • Economy 5%
  • Social Issues 5%

Obejective

  • Topic: 120-180 words of relevant discussion with reasoning, example, and preferably concession
  • Coherence: Clear topic, flow of idea, adn connection between sentences
  • Language: Various, accurate and natural
  • Grammar: A mix of simple and complex sentences, preferably with atrributive clauses and participles

Structure - evaluation/comparison

  • Point & Elaboraiton + Further Elaboration + Example + Concession
  • Point & Elaboration + Further Elaboration + Seconde Point & Elaboration + Further Elaboration
  • Point & Elaboration + Further Elaboration + Full Concession
  • Point & Elaboration + Further Elaboration + Sublimation

  2. Personal development / Career development
  3. Teamworking and communication skills
